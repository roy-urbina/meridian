Help & Guide

Everything you need to use Meridian effectively

📊Dashboard
What does the Dashboard show?
Your financial snapshot at a glance: net worth across all accounts, this month's income vs expenses, top budget categories, fund balances, and a 6-month income/expense trend chart.
What is the 6-Month Trend chart?
Side-by-side bars for each of the last 6 months. Lime green = income, red = expenses. The current month is fully saturated; past months are faded. Bars grow proportionally — the tallest bar always fills the full height.
How do I go to a full report?
Click Full → in the top-right of the trend chart, or navigate to Reports in the sidebar.
The Dashboard auto-refreshes every time you navigate to it. Pull-to-refresh is not needed.
📒Transactions
How do I add a transaction?
Press N or click ＋ Add Row at the bottom of the table. Fill in Account, Date, Payee, Category, and either Payment (money out) or Deposit (money in). Press Tab to move between cells. The row saves automatically when you leave it.
What is Quick Add (Q)?
Press Q from anywhere in the app to open the Quick Add modal. Enter the amount, payee, account, and category — press Enter to save without navigating away. Perfect for capturing expenses on the fly.
What do the cleared status codes mean?
— (blank) = Uncleared / not yet on your statement.
C = Cleared — the transaction has appeared on your bank statement but reconciliation is not yet complete.
R = Reconciled — fully matched and locked during a reconciliation session. Click the pill to cycle through statuses.
How do I split a transaction across categories?
Click the ⊕ button in the action column of any transaction. A modal opens where you add as many category lines as needed with amounts. The modal shows a live balance indicator — green ✓ when it matches the total, amber when there's a remainder. Split amounts count toward the correct budget categories and reports.
How does payee memory work?
When you select a payee from the autocomplete dropdown, Meridian automatically fills in the Category and Fund from the last time you used that payee. This saves time for recurring merchants like grocery stores, gas stations, etc.
What does FLAG in the memo do?
Type FLAG (all caps) in any memo cell to flag that transaction for follow-up. The row highlights in gold and a pulsing 🚩 Flagged button appears at the top of the Transactions page showing how many are flagged. Click it to open a popup with all flagged transactions — you can edit the memo directly or click Clear Flag to remove it.
How do I bulk-edit transactions?
Check the checkbox in the leftmost column of one or more transaction rows. A floating action bar appears at the bottom of the screen with options to: change the category for all selected, change the fund, or delete all selected at once. The checkbox in the table header selects/deselects all visible rows.
How does the memo/notes field work?
Click any cell in the Memo column to expand it into a textarea. Type your note (any length), then press Escape or click elsewhere to save. The cell shows the first 60 characters with an ellipsis. Hover to see the full text in a tooltip.
How do I find a specific transaction number?
Use the global search bar (press / to focus it) and type: Tx #89, #89, or just 89. The result shows the transaction's payee, date, account, category, fund, and amount. Clicking the result jumps to Transactions and highlights that row in gold for 2 seconds.
How do I search for all transfers?
Type transfer in the global search bar to see all transfer transactions.
Transactions are sorted newest-first by default. The Balance column always shows the running balance for that specific account, regardless of what account filter is active.
🏦Accounts
What account types does Meridian support?
Checking, Savings, Cash, Credit Card, and Loan. Assets (checking, savings, cash) appear in the top table. Liabilities (credit cards, loans) appear below it. Net Worth is calculated as total assets minus total liabilities.
What does the Reconcile Status column show?
Each account shows how many transactions are uncleared (⚠) and the date of the last reconciliation session. Green ✓ means all transactions are cleared. This helps you spot accounts that haven't been reconciled recently.
How do I reconcile an account?
Click Reconcile on any account row. Enter your statement's ending balance, then check off each transaction that appears on the statement. When the difference reaches $0.00, click Mark Reconciled to lock those transactions as R. If the statement doesn't balance (missing transactions), use Reconcile Checked ⚠ to reconcile what you have — a note shows the remaining difference.
💼Funds
What are Funds?
Virtual envelopes — earmarked money for a specific purpose (Emergency Fund, Vacation, Car Repair, etc.). The money still lives in your bank account, but Funds track how much of it is "spoken for." Great for saving toward goals without opening separate accounts.
What does the Funds page header show?
Three summary cards: Monthly Recurring Committed — all active recurring payments normalized to a monthly cost (weekly bills × 52/12, yearly bills ÷ 12, etc.); Total Funds Balance — sum of all fund balances; Budget by Group — current month actual vs budget for each expense category group (021, 022, etc.). Click the Monthly Recurring number to expand a detailed per-category breakdown.
How do auto-replenishment rules work?
Set up rules under a specific account that automatically allocate a percentage of each deposit into your funds. When you post a deposit, the replenishment preview appears — review the allocation, then click Apply. If you delete that deposit transaction later, the fund allocations are automatically removed too.
How do I transfer money between funds?
Click the action button (⋯) on any fund row and select Transfer to Fund. Choose the source and destination funds and the amount — the money moves between envelopes without affecting your actual bank balance.
🎯Budget
How is the budget structured?
Categories follow a 4-digit code system: 01xx = Income, 02xx = Expenses. The 3rd digit is the group (021 = Housing, 022 = Utilities, 025 = Vehicle, etc.). The 4th digit is the specific line item. This lets you search and filter at both the group level (029) and individual line level (0291).
What is budget rollover?
Check the Rollover checkbox on any expense category row. If you spend less than budgeted in a month, the surplus automatically adds to next month's budget for that category. For example: budget $200 for groceries in January, spend $150 — February's grocery budget becomes $250.
What is the Uncategorized row?
An amber ⚠ row that appears when you have expenses with no category assigned. It shows the total and count — click the "view" link to filter transactions to just uncategorized ones so you can fix them.
How does the Yearly Budget view work?
Toggle between Budget, Actual, and Difference views. In Difference mode: positive numbers = surplus (you came in under budget for expenses, or over budget for income), negative = deficit. Positive differences are green; negative are red.
Do split transactions count toward the budget?
Yes. Each line of a split transaction is counted toward its respective category in both the monthly and yearly budget views.
🔁Recurring Transactions
What frequencies are supported?
Weekly, Biweekly, Monthly, Yearly, and Custom (every N days). The Funds page normalizes all of these to a monthly cost for the summary header.
What is the difference between Post and Skip?
Post creates a transaction from the recurring rule and advances the due date to the next occurrence. Skip advances the due date without creating a transaction — use this when you paid it outside the app or it didn't occur that period.
How do I see what's due?
A red badge on the Recurring nav item shows how many items are due. Due items appear at the top of the Recurring page in an alert row. The Dashboard also shows a "Due" pill in the header when items are overdue.
📈Reports
How do I change the report period?
Use the ‹ › arrows to step through months, or click the month label to pick from a calendar. The dropdown lets you switch between This Month, Last 3 Months, Last 6 Months, This Year, or Custom Range.
What does the Spending by Category chart show?
Top 10 expense categories for the selected period, ranked by amount. Each bar shows the dollar amount and percentage of total spending. Toggle between This Period and Year to Date. Split transaction amounts are counted toward the correct category.
What is the Income Breakdown section?
Shows gross earnings, total deductions, net deposited, and effective tax rate for the period — populated from Income Breakdown entries you save when posting deposits. Each paycheck is listed in the log on the right.
How do I export to PDF?
Click ⬇ Export PDF. A new tab opens with a formatted financial report including: Executive Summary (net worth, income, expenses, savings rate), Budget vs Actual for the period, Income Breakdown (if logged), top spending categories, and the full transaction statement. Print it or save as PDF from your browser.
What does the category group search show?
Searching for a 3-digit prefix like 029 shows a popup with the aggregate for that entire group — total actual, total budget, and a per-subcategory breakdown with progress bars. Searching for a 4-digit code like 0291 shows just that specific line item.
💳Debt Payoff
What strategies are available?
❄ Snowball: Pay minimums on everything, put extra toward the smallest balance first. Psychological wins from eliminating debts quickly. 🏔 Avalanche: Put extra toward the highest interest rate first. Mathematically optimal — pays the least total interest. Minimums Only: Baseline comparison showing what happens if you only pay minimums.
How does the Extra Monthly Payment work?
Any amount you enter is added on top of the sum of all minimum payments and applied to your target debt according to the chosen strategy. Everything recalculates live as you type.
Are the balances connected to my accounts?
Yes. On first load, the Debt page auto-imports all Credit and Loan accounts from your Accounts page with their current balances. If you link a debt entry to an account (via the acctId field), the balance syncs automatically each time you open the page.
What does the Interest Comparison show?
Side-by-side bars comparing your chosen strategy against minimums-only — showing total interest paid and months to debt-free for each. The difference is the money you save by using a payoff strategy.
☁️Sync & Data Safety
Where is my data stored?
All data lives in your browser's localStorage on your device. Nothing is sent anywhere without your action. The app works completely offline.
How do I back up my data?
Configure GitHub Gist sync in the ☁️ Sync Data button at the bottom of the sidebar. Enter your GitHub personal access token (with gist scope) and a Gist ID. Push manually or enable Auto-Sync (3-second debounce after each change).
What happens if I clear my browser data?
Your data will be lost unless you have GitHub Gist sync configured. It's strongly recommended to set up sync and push at least once a week.
How do I access Setup Wizard again?
Click ⚙ Setup Wizard at the very bottom of the sidebar. You'll be prompted for the password (meridian by default — change it in the script). The wizard opens without erasing existing data; changes only save when you click Finish & Launch.
Saves are debounced — the app waits 400ms after your last change before writing to storage. The "Saving…" indicator shows immediately for responsiveness. Auto-sync waits 3 seconds after a save before pushing to GitHub to avoid hitting rate limits.
💰Income Breakdown & Tax Tracking
What is the Income Breakdown popup?
After saving a deposit transaction (or after applying a fund replenishment), a popup appears letting you log: Gross Amount, and individual deduction lines (Federal Tax, State Tax, Social Security, Medicare, and any custom lines). The Net amount is auto-filled from the transaction. A warning appears if Gross − Deductions ≠ Net.
How do I view a saved breakdown?
Transactions with a saved breakdown show a green 💰 button in the action column. Click it for a popup showing gross, deducted, and net with each deduction line and its % of gross. Click Edit Breakdown to modify it.
Where does this appear in Reports?
The Income Breakdown card in Reports aggregates all breakdowns in the selected period: total gross, total deducted, total net, effective tax rate, and a per-line deduction table (e.g. total Federal Tax paid year-to-date).

☁️ Sync Data — GitHub Gist

Backup and sync your Meridian data across devices
How this works: Your data is saved as a private file on your GitHub account (called a Gist). Only you can see it. To sync between devices, open Meridian on each device and paste the same token and Gist ID.

One-time setup: Create a free GitHub account, then generate a personal access token with Gist permissions. Takes about 2 minutes. See the instructions below.
This is stored only in your browser's localStorage and never sent anywhere except GitHub's own API.
📖 Step-by-step setup instructions ▶
Step 1 — Create a GitHub account
Go to github.com and sign up for a free account if you don't have one. Confirm your email.

Step 2 — Generate a Personal Access Token
1. Click your profile picture (top right) → Settings
2. Scroll all the way down → click Developer settings
3. Click Personal access tokens → Tokens (classic)
4. Click Generate new token → Generate new token (classic)
5. Give it a name like "Meridian Sync"
6. Set expiration to "No expiration" (or however long you want)
7. Check the gist checkbox — that's the only permission needed
8. Click Generate token at the bottom
9. Copy the token immediately — GitHub only shows it once. It starts with "ghp_"

Step 3 — Paste the token above and click Push to Cloud
Leave the Gist ID blank on your first sync. Meridian will create a new private Gist automatically and show you the ID to save.

Step 4 — Set up on your other devices
Open Meridian on your phone or another computer. Paste the same token and the Gist ID that was created in Step 3. Click Pull from Cloud to load your data.
